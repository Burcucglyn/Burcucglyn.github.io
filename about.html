About

The longer version.

How I got here and what I'm looking for next.

I started in environmental engineering, where I learned that real-world data is messy by default and that the work before the analysis often determines whether the results mean anything. Three years in marketing taught me something different: how to translate numbers into decisions for people who don't care about the method, only the outcome.

The MSc in Data Science at Birkbeck brought the technical depth. My dissertation trained 556 machine learning models across London's air quality monitoring networks, but the finding I'm most proud of is a simple one: the previous hour's reading explains almost everything. Sometimes the honest answer is that the problem is simpler than it looks.

What I want to do next is build prediction systems that work reliably and translate complex data into something any audience can act on. My background in environmental engineering gives me domain context, but the approach applies wherever messy data meets real decisions — climate, health, finance, or otherwise.

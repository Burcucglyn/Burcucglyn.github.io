Things I've built.

London Air Quality Prediction ↗

MSc dissertation. Trained 556 models (Random Forest and CNN) across London's air quality monitoring networks: LAQN (78 stations, 6 regulatory pollutants) and DEFRA (18 stations, 37 pollutants). Compared both networks against shared regulatory pollutants, with an extended comparison across DEFRA's full pollutant set. Built a three-stage cleaning pipeline that reduced LAQN metadata issues from 47.5% to 17.2%. The key finding from the Random Forest models: t-1 lag features explain 85-95% of prediction variance. Hourly air quality prediction is fundamentally a persistence problem, and data quality (DEFRA, 91.2% complete) consistently beat data quantity (LAQN, 141 site-pollutant combinations).

Python scikit-learn TensorFlow Pandas

Data engineering pipeline for YouTube audio collection and engagement analysis. Built serial, parallel, and rate-limited download modes with thread-safe logging. Parallel mode ran 5x faster. Ran identical queries in both Pandas and PySpark to compare behaviour on the same dataset.
